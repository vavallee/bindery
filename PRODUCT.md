# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Bindery is for self-hosted media and library operators who manage ebooks and audiobooks through indexers, download clients, metadata providers, and local storage. They use the UI during setup, troubleshooting, and repeated library maintenance, often while checking whether authors, books, queues, imports, and metadata are in the expected state.

## Product Purpose

Bindery replaces abandoned or fragile book automation workflows with a reliable single-binary manager for monitoring authors, finding releases, downloading, importing, organizing, and exposing books through OPDS. Success means users can understand current library state, configure integrations, recover from failures, and add books or authors with confidence.

## Brand Personality

Calm, technical, reliable. The product should feel like a capable operations console for a personal library: clear about consequences, compact enough for repeat use, and precise about automation state.

## Anti-references

Avoid promotional SaaS presentation, decorative dashboards that obscure status, playful reader-app styling that hides operational controls, and vague copy that does not explain what an action will do. Avoid UX patterns that resemble fragile scraper workflows: ambiguous search results, hidden failure states, and add flows that leave users unsure what will be monitored or grabbed.

## Design Principles

1. Make automation legible before asking for commitment.
2. Keep repeated operations compact, scannable, and keyboard-friendly.
3. Show consequences at the point of action, especially for monitoring, grabbing, importing, deletion, and metadata changes.
4. Prefer explicit state over decorative emphasis.
5. Preserve trust by surfacing errors and partial success clearly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and interaction patterns. Preserve light and dark theme support, visible focus states, keyboard access, readable placeholder text, and reduced-motion alternatives for any animation. Design for long lists, narrow mobile screens, low-vision contrast needs, and users who may be working from a server dashboard or remote admin session.
